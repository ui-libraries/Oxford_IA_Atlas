Land Ownership in Oxford, Iowa: 1859 - 1953


Research and map by Matthew Noellert, PhD, et al. | University of Iowa Digital Scholarship & Publishing Studio, 2020 | Works Cited | Main Page

This is a map of land ownership and family origins for Oxford Township, Iowa from 1859 to 1953. This map accompanies the Digital Oxford Township project, which was created by students in the University of Iowa (UI) Department of History’s Spring 2020 course, HIST:2195 Digital History Workshop, in collaboration with the State Historical Society of Iowa and the UI Library’s Digital Scholarship and Publishing Studio. Please visit here for details on the sources for this map, and here for more information on the construction and analysis of its data. This map is designed to visualize the spatial features of land ownership and how they changed over time. Users can explore the family origins and types of landowners, and the concentration of land ownership over the first century of Oxford’s history. Users can also view the land of specific family names or click on individual land parcels for more information. Please note that the base map displays orthoimagery from 1930.
